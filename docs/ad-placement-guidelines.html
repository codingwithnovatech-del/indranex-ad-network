Ad Placement Guidelines & Best Practices

Last updated: June 24, 2026

Proper ad placement is critical to maximizing your revenue while maintaining a positive user experience. The following guidelines outline how Indranex AdNet ads should be placed on your website. Adherence to these rules ensures compliance with our network policies and prevents account penalties.

General Placement Rules

Each page may display a maximum of three ad slots simultaneously. This includes all format types display banners, popunders, interstitials, and social bars combined. Exceeding this limit may result in ad serving being disabled on your account.

Ad slots must not overlap, stack, or cover one another. Maintain a minimum spacing of 300 pixels between any two ad containers on the same page to ensure readability and avoid accidental clicks.

Above-the-Fold & Below-the-Fold Placements

Above-the-fold placements (visible without scrolling) typically yield higher CPM rates. However, we recommend a balanced approach: place one above-the-fold slot and one or two below-the-fold slots. Avoid placing more than one ad above the fold so that content remains the primary focus for visitors.

Responsive Design Requirements

All ad containers must adapt fluidly across device sizes. Our ad script automatically serves responsive creative units, but your container elements must use percentage-based or flexible widths. Test your layout on mobile, tablet, and desktop viewports to confirm proper rendering. Ads that break page layouts, overflow containers, or cause horizontal scrolling violate our placement policy.

Popunder Triggers

Popunder ads may only be triggered by explicit user interaction such as a click, tap, or scroll. Automatically firing popunders on page load, mouse movement, or timer-based events is strictly prohibited. The triggered popunder must open a new tab or window behind the current browsing context.

Interstitial Frequency Capping

Full-screen interstitial ads may be served a maximum of once per user session. Implement session tracking on your end to prevent repeated interstitial displays to the same visitor. Interstitials should appear at natural navigation breaks, such as between page transitions.

Social Bar Positioning

The social bar widget should be positioned at the edge of the viewport, either left or right side, without obstructing main content. It must not cover interactive elements like buttons, forms, or navigation menus. Provide users the ability to dismiss the social bar permanently.

Prohibited Placements

The following ad placements are strictly forbidden and will result in immediate account suspension:

  • Pop-up ads that appear in front of content or require dismissal before content access
  • Deceptive close buttons that are difficult to locate or trigger unintended ad clicks
  • Ads placed inside iframes that are hidden, transparent, or zero-sized
  • Forcing users to view or interact with ads to access content (ad walls)
  • Ads placed on error pages, under-construction pages, or pages with no substantive content
  • Injection of ads into third-party websites without authorization (ad injection)

Best Practices for Maximizing Revenue

Test different ad positions using our A/B testing dashboard. Monitor your click-through rate (CTR) and eCPM metrics regularly. Place in-article ads between paragraphs of long-form content for higher engagement. Use the sidebar for sticky ad placements on desktop. Always prioritize user experience aggressive ad placements drive visitors away and harm long-term revenue.
