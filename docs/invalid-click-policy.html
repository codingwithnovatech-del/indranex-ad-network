Invalid Click & Fraud Prevention Policy

Last updated: June 24, 2026

Indranex AdNet maintains a zero-tolerance approach to invalid click activity and ad fraud. Our fraud prevention systems protect advertisers from paying for fake engagement and ensure publishers are rewarded fairly for legitimate traffic. Understanding this policy is essential for every publisher in our network.

What Constitutes an Invalid Click?

Invalid clicks are any ad interactions that do not represent genuine user interest. The following activities are classified as invalid clicks:

  • Accidental Double Clicks: Multiple rapid clicks on the same ad unit by the same user within a short timeframe.
  • Automated Clicking: Any use of scripts, macros, or software programs designed to simulate ad clicks.
  • Click Bots: Automated programs or malware that generate clicks without human involvement.
  • Click Farms: Large groups of low-paid workers manually clicking ads to inflate earnings.
  • Self-Clicks: A publisher clicking on their own ads or instructing others to do so.
  • Incentivized Clicks: Offering compensation, prizes, or services in exchange for ad clicks.
  • Forced Clicks: Implementing deceptive UI elements that cause unintentional ad interactions, such as hidden overlays or misleading close buttons.
  • Excessive Clicking: Repeated clicking on ads by the same IP address, device, or user account beyond normal user behavior patterns.

Detection Methods

Our fraud detection infrastructure uses multiple layers of analysis to identify invalid activity in real time:

  • IP Analysis: We track IP addresses and flag activity from datacenters, known proxy servers, VPN endpoints, or suspicious geolocations.
  • User-Agent Fingerprinting: Our system analyzes browser signatures to detect inconsistencies or non-standard user agents typically associated with automated tools.
  • Session Tracking: We monitor user sessions including click timing, page navigation patterns, and engagement depth to distinguish human behavior from automated activity.
  • Time-Pattern Analysis: Machine learning algorithms identify unnatural click patterns such as precisely timed intervals, extremely high click frequency, or clicks occurring 24/7 without breaks.
  • Device Fingerprinting: We generate unique device identifiers to detect click farms operating from the same hardware with rotating IP addresses.

Consequences of Invalid Click Activity

Depending on the severity and frequency of violations, we apply escalating enforcement actions:

  • Warning: Minor first-time violations result in a formal warning and a 30-day monitoring period.
  • Payment Hold: Suspicious activity triggers a temporary hold on all outstanding payments pending investigation (typically 30-60 days).
  • Account Suspension: Repeated or moderate violations lead to account suspension and forfeiture of any unpaid balance.
  • Permanent Ban: Severe or intentional fraud results in permanent blacklisting from the Indranex AdNet network.
  • Legal Action: Cases involving significant financial fraud may be reported to law enforcement agencies for criminal prosecution.

Appeal Process

If your account has been flagged for invalid click activity and you believe it was done in error, you may submit an appeal within 14 days of notification. Provide a detailed explanation of your traffic sources, any third-party tools you use, and any evidence supporting your case. Our review team will re-evaluate the data and respond within 10 business days. Frivolous or repeated appeals may be disregarded.
