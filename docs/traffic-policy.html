Traffic Quality Policy

Last updated: June 24, 2026

Indranex AdNet is committed to providing a high-quality advertising ecosystem where advertisers receive genuine engagement and publishers are rewarded for real traffic. This policy outlines what constitutes acceptable and unacceptable traffic sources on our network.

Our Commitment to Quality Traffic

We believe that authentic, human-generated traffic is the foundation of a sustainable advertising model. Our systems are designed to identify, measure, and reward genuine user engagement while filtering out artificial or low-quality traffic. Publishers who focus on creating valuable content and attracting real visitors will always thrive on our platform.

Allowed Traffic Sources

The following traffic sources are fully compliant with our policy, provided they direct real human visitors to your site:

  • Organic Search: Visitors arriving from search engines like Google, Bing, or DuckDuckGo.
  • Social Media: Traffic from legitimate social platforms such as Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, LinkedIn, Reddit, Pinterest, and YouTube.
  • Email Campaigns: Subscribers who click through from your newsletters or email marketing campaigns.
  • SEO Traffic: Visitors who find your site through search engine optimization efforts and relevant keyword rankings.
  • Paid Advertising: Traffic from legitimate ad campaigns (Google Ads, Facebook Ads, native advertising) provided clicks originate from real users and not bots or click farms.
  • Direct Traffic: Visitors who type your URL directly into their browser or use a bookmark.
  • Referral Traffic: Visitors from legitimate partner sites, guest posts, or backlink sources.

Prohibited Traffic Sources and Practices

The following activities are strictly forbidden and will result in immediate account suspension, forfeiture of all earnings, and potential legal action:

  • Bots and Automated Scripts: Any use of automated software, bots, crawlers, or scripts to generate fake impressions or clicks.
  • Auto-Refresh Traffic: Using meta refresh tags, JavaScript timers, or iframe auto-reloading to generate repeated page views.
  • Click Farms: Paying or contracting individuals to manually click on ads or visit pages.
  • Traffic Exchange Programs: Participating in any network where users visit each other's sites in exchange for traffic credits.
  • Incentivized Clicking: Offering rewards, payments, or any form of compensation to users for clicking ads or visiting pages.
  • VPN/Proxy Traffic: Routing traffic through VPNs, proxies, or anonymizers to disguise geographic location.
  • Crawling/Scraping: Allowing or enabling automated tools to crawl or scrape your site for the purpose of generating ad impressions.
  • Pop-up/Pop-under Generators: Using third-party tools that generate unwanted windows or tabs that load your site with ads.

Traffic Monitoring and Analytics

We employ advanced traffic analysis systems that monitor visitor behavior in real time. Our detection engine evaluates metrics including session duration, pages per session, mouse movements, click patterns, IP reputation, user-agent consistency, and geolocation data. Suspicious patterns are flagged automatically, and accounts under review may have payments temporarily held until an investigation is completed.

Violations and Consequences

If our systems detect prohibited traffic sources or patterns, we issue a warning for first-time minor violations. Continued or severe violations lead to immediate account suspension and forfeiture of unpaid earnings. Chronic offenders are permanently banned from the network. We also reserve the right to report fraudulent activity to relevant authorities.
