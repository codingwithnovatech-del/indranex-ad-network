How Display Ads Work: A Complete Guide for Publishers

Last updated: June 24, 2026

Display advertising is the backbone of modern website monetization. When you visit a website and see a banner promoting a product or service, you are witnessing display advertising in action. This guide breaks down the entire ecosystem so publishers can understand exactly how money flows from advertisers to their bank accounts.

The Display Advertising Ecosystem

At its simplest, display advertising involves three key players. The advertiser wants to promote their product and is willing to pay for visibility. The publisher owns a website with space to show ads. The ad network acts as the middleman, matching advertiser demand with publisher supply in real time. When a user visits a publisher's page, an ad request is sent to the network, which runs a lightning-fast auction and returns the winning creative — all within milliseconds.

Common Ad Formats

Publishers can choose from several ad formats depending on their site layout and audience preferences. Banner ads are rectangular display units placed above, beside, or within content. Interstitial ads cover the full screen at natural break points, such as between page transitions. Native ads match the look and feel of the surrounding content, delivering a less intrusive experience. Popunder ads open in a new window behind the current browser tab and offer high CPM rates while remaining unobtrusive during the session.

How Impressions and Clicks Are Tracked

Every ad impression and click is tracked through a combination of pixel tracking and server-side logging. When an ad loads, a 1x1 transparent pixel fires, recording the impression. Clicks are tracked via a redirect URL that logs the click event before sending the user to the advertiser's landing page. Indranex AdNet uses Firebase-based real-time event tracking, ensuring accurate, fraud-resistant measurement that both publishers and advertisers can trust.

Real-Time Bidding vs. Direct Deals

In Real-Time Bidding (RTB), advertisers bid for each impression individually through an auction. This maximizes fill rates and ensures every impression is sold to the highest bidder. Direct deals involve a fixed-price agreement between a publisher and advertiser for a guaranteed number of impressions. Many publishers use a hybrid approach: direct deals for premium inventory and RTB for the rest. Indranex AdNet specializes in RTB with intelligent floor pricing to protect publisher revenue.

The Role of Ad Servers and SDKs

Ad servers store and deliver creatives, while SDKs (software development kits) integrate ad functionality into websites and apps. Indranex AdNet provides a lightweight JavaScript tag that publishers embed in their pages. This tag communicates with our Firebase-powered backend to fetch the highest-paying ad in real time. The entire process is optimized for speed, ensuring your site's Core Web Vitals remain strong.
