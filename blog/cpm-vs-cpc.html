CPM vs CPC: Which Pricing Model is Best for Your Website?

Last updated: June 24, 2026

Choosing the right pricing model is one of the most important decisions a publisher can make. The two dominant models — CPM and CPC — work very differently and each suits particular types of websites and traffic. Understanding the difference will help you maximize your ad revenue.

What is CPM?

CPM stands for Cost Per Mille, where "mille" is Latin for thousand. Under this model, advertisers pay for every 1,000 impressions their ad receives, regardless of whether anyone clicks. For example, a $5 CPM means the advertiser pays $5 every time the ad is shown 1,000 times. Publishers earn a share of this revenue. CPM is ideal for high-traffic content websites, news portals, and blogs where the goal is to generate broad brand awareness. Typical CPM rates range from $0.50 to $5.00 depending on niche and geography.

What is CPC?

CPC stands for Cost Per Click. Here, advertisers only pay when a user actually clicks on the ad. This model is performance-based and appeals to advertisers who want direct actions like purchases or sign-ups. For publishers, CPC can be more volatile because earnings depend on user engagement, not just traffic volume. Typical CPC rates range from $0.10 to $1.00. CPC works best for websites with highly targeted, conversion-ready audiences, such as review sites or comparison pages.

Which Model Should You Choose?

If your website generates large volumes of traffic and your content naturally keeps visitors on the page, CPM is the better choice. Content sites, entertainment portals, and informational blogs tend to earn more with CPM because they can monetize every page view. On the other hand, if you run a niche site with a small but highly engaged audience that clicks on relevant offers, CPC can deliver better returns.
